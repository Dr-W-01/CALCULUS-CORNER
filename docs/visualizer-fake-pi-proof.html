FAKE PROOF: π = 4

Description

This classic “proof” builds a staircase of horizontal and vertical segments around a unit circle. Each refinement adds more steps, and the shape visually converges to the circle — yet the total path length stays exactly 4 (the perimeter of the circumscribing square). The visual convergence tricks the eye; the perimeter does not approach 2π. It is a vivid reminder that limits of curves are not limits of their lengths: pointwise convergence does not guarantee convergence of arc length or perimeter.
