Description & Behavior

The natural logarithm is the inverse of e^x. Its domain restriction to positive x creates a vertical asymptote at x = 0.

Limits and Continuity

End Behaviours

The function grows without bound as x → ∞ and plunges to −∞ as x approaches 0 from the right.

Limit at a Point

Only the right-hand limit at x = 0 exists (as −∞), matching the vertical asymptote.

General Limit

This function is continuous on (0, ∞) with an infinite discontinuity at x = 0.

What the Derivatives Tell Us

The derivative 1/x is always positive on the domain, so ln(x) increases everywhere with no critical points. The second derivative is always negative, giving concave-down behavior throughout.
