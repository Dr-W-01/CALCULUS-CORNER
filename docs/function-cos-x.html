Description & Behavior

Cosine is the even companion to sine, with the same period but a phase shift. It has infinitely many critical points and inflection points.

Limits and Continuity

End Behaviours

Cosine oscillates between −1 and 1, so there is no horizontal asymptote and no limit at ±∞.

General Limit

This function is continuous at all real numbers.

What the Derivatives Tell Us

Critical points occur where sin(x) = 0 — local maxima at even multiples of π and local minima at odd multiples. Inflection points at (π/2 + kπ, 0) come from f'' changing sign.
